How we engage

Four steps. No surprises. No bait‑and‑switch.

Every Buoy engagement runs the same way — whether it's a six-week implementation, a three-month process improvement, or a long-running same-day support arrangement. Fixed scope, senior consultant, full transparency. Here's what that looks like end-to-end.

01
Discovery · 30–60 minutes · no cost

We listen first.

A no-cost call. We listen, ask questions, take notes. We learn your current Jobpac setup, what's hurting most, and what's already worked. You're talking to the senior consultant who'd actually do the work — not a sales person reading off a script.

You walk away with a clear sense of whether we're a fit. If we're not, we'll say so — and where possible, point you at someone who is.

02
Scope · 3–7 business days

You get a fixed-scope proposal.

Within a week, you receive a written proposal: deliverables, timeline, price, and the named senior consultant who'll lead the engagement. No "T&E to be confirmed". No "phase 2 to be quoted later". The price is the price.

You review, push back on scope, ask questions. We adjust. Sign-off on scope before any work starts — so there's no ambiguity about what's in or out.

03
Execute · duration varies by scope

Senior consultant on the keys.

The person on the proposal does the work. Not a junior. Not a partner who hands it down. Weekly written status, risks called out the week they emerge — not the week before go-live.

If anything material changes mid-engagement — new requirement, scope creep, surprise data issue — we agree it explicitly with you in writing before doing it. No surprise invoices.

04
Embed · final 1–2 weeks of every engagement

Your team owns it when we leave.

Documentation: workflow guides, configuration notes, training material — written so your team can run it long after we're gone. Hands-on training with the people who'll use the system day-to-day, in your real environment.

A structured handover so nothing slips. Optional ongoing same-day support after handover — most clients want a thread to pull on for the first 90 days, but you're never locked in.

Common questions

What people usually ask before signing.

What if scope changes mid-engagement?

We pause and agree the change in writing before doing it. You'll see the impact on price and timeline before you commit. If we don't agree on the change, we deliver against the original scope and the new requirement becomes a separate engagement.

Do you charge for discovery?

No. The first call is free, and the written proposal that follows is also free. We only charge once you've signed off on a scope.

How fast can you start?

For most support engagements, same week. For larger implementations or process improvements, the typical lead time is two to four weeks because we plan around current commitments. If something is genuinely urgent, tell us and we'll work out what's possible.

Do you work in person or remotely?

Both. We're based in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, and Auckland, and we travel anywhere in AU/NZ for on-site work. A typical implementation has a mix of on-site and remote days; ongoing support is mostly remote with site visits when something needs hands-on.

What size of business is Buoy a fit for?

Most clients are between $20m and $500m turnover — mid-market construction and civil businesses with one finance function. Smaller and we're usually overkill. Larger and there's typically an internal team that needs different kinds of help. Either way, the discovery call will tell us.

Can we hire you on retainer?

Yes — that's what our same-day support service is. A defined number of hours per week or month, used flexibly across the team. Most clients start with a project, then move to a support retainer afterwards.

Ready to start with a discovery call?

30 minutes, no slide deck, no obligation. Senior consultant on the line, not a sales person.
